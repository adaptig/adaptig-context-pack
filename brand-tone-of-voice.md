# Brand Tone of Voice

Adaptig's voice is narrative, anti-hype, and concrete. We sound like practitioners who teach, not consultants who pitch. Every claim we make is grounded in something we have shipped, delivered, or observed directly.

## Core principles

**Accuracy over speed.** We would rather be slow and right than fast and hedging. Numbers are specific when specifics exist — "4,800+ participants across 120+ workshops," not "thousands of professionals."

**Show, don't claim.** We never say "as experts in AI adoption." We say "when we trained 4,800+ participants across 3 continents" or name the specific engagement. Expertise proves itself through specific examples, not adjectives.

**Anti-hype, always.** We do not use words like leverage, innovative, cutting-edge, empower, unlock, game-changer, or deep dive. If a sentence could appear on any AI company's homepage, it does not belong in ours.

**Outcome-first: sell the flow, not the tool.** We describe what changes for the practitioner, not the feature list of the software.

## Words we use

workflow, adoption, practitioners, friction, shipped, built, landed, layer, the gap, engagement (for client work).

## Words we avoid

leverage, innovative, cutting-edge, state-of-the-art, empower, unlock potential, democratize, disruption, thought leader, game-changer, deep dive (as a noun), at the end of the day, moving forward, "in today's rapidly changing landscape."

## Format preferences

No emojis in Adaptig-branded content. Concise narrative paragraphs, not bullet soup. Lead with what changed, what you need, and what is next — in that order. Em-dashes are "—", not " - " or "–".

## Register across channels

**Client-facing materials** (proposals, emails, reports, invoices, keynotes): English, professional, first person, direct. Short declarative sentences. Fragments for emphasis. Specific numbers when they exist.

**Internal team communication** (Roam, meeting notes, Slack): Same principles, slightly looser cadence. The anti-hype rule still applies — we do not pitch each other.

**Public classes and training materials**: Teaching cadence. Frameworks reduce cognitive load; start small, then scale complexity. Every step names a specific tool — never "use an AI tool," always "open NotebookLM" or "run Claude Code."

## Out of scope

This file covers Adaptig's corporate voice only. Team members writing personal content on personal channels follow their own voice — this file applies to Adaptig-branded communication only.
